UMB Centers & Institutes Task Force Survey

About the Task Force

The UMB Centers & Institutes Task Force is tasked with surveying the current stable of Centers and Institutes withing the university.

The main charge of the task force is to:

  • Classification of Centers and Institutes (C&I): discussion of what the types of C&I might be like here, what are some of the elements or characteristics of these, how are they organized and what types of financial structures would be used to support them.
  • Funding options: what are some of the possible funding options for C&I, what are some of the incentives that should be considered in seeking alternative funding, what are the activities that may lead to alternative as well as core university support for C&I, what university resources could be available (university advancement, ORSP, other)
  • Evaluation guidelines for C&I: what should periodic evaluation strategies for C&I be, including but not limited to criteria, frequency and fiscal resources as well as mission relevance and contributions to the University

These parallel the directives in the survey which are:

  • A definitional document that classifies centers and institutes, differentiating among types of mission (e.g., teaching centers, research centers, outreach/service centers, fee-based centers);
  • Recommended guidelines for funding options for centers and institutes, differentiated by mission-type; these guidelines should address such questions as:
    • To what extent should each mission-type be expected to achieve fiscal self-sufficiency through external funds, versus to what extent certain activities of a center or institute should be supported by internal funds or in-kind contributions;
    • Where a center or institute's mission crosses boundaries among types, the principles on which its costs should be apportioned among different sources of funds (e.g., external funds, development funds, the general operating budget of the university);
